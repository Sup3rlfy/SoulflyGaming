About Me

  • I have always loved sports, not watching, but playing soccer and running. Later on, I traded them for biking and swimming.
  • Early on in grammar school, I got hooked on Dungeons and Dragons and fantasy worlds in general. This love expanded into science fiction with the arrival of Command and Conquer and the Starship Troopers movie.
  • I always loved watching those movies and playing games set in those worlds, and for a long time I imagined myself writing such stories.

    • ‎

  • After grammar school in 2001, I had to make a decision: study Economics or Computer Science. Since I had always done well in math, but the one elective programming class had always left me with a headache, I chose Economics. (Well, and maybe the idea of learning how to invest my own money once I made some played a role too.)
  • Long story short: I chose wrongly and was bored out of my mind by Business Economics, until I barely attended classes and spent my time working various jobs instead.
  • Eventually, I realized my studies weren't going anywhere and applied for a job as a heavy transport driver at our local steel plant.

    • ‎

  • After about a decade in a dead-end job, I caught myself having spent five to six hours every day after work for half a year trying to teach myself JavaScript and C# through YouTube tutorials and hobby projects.
  • At that point, I also realized that coding is as close to real magic, not the Houdini-cutting-someone-in-half kind of magic, as anyone could realistically get. This realization further fueled my love for programming.
  • So a change hat to be made, and as I learned there was even a program to pay for my Bachelor's degree there was no stopping me.
  • Three and a bit years later, I am nearly done with my degree in Computer Sciences, only my thesis and one Analysis class are missing, and I am ready to see what the job market has to offer.
  • In retrospect, this was one of the best decisions in my life. I am still soo happy I did this.

    • ‎

  • My goal now is to get a good, secure job that also brings me closer to my dream:
  • First, to build my own single-player game and introduce my ideas to the world, followed by a massive multiplayer experience set in a similar universe.
  • So I am keen on any job that gradually introduces me to, helps me improve my knowledge of, or involves working with:
  • Database implementation and management, data warehousing, user management and session handling, cash shops, C++, client-server or cloud systems and probably many more things I can’t even imagine yet.
